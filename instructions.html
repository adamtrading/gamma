Setup Guide

How to import your
Quantum data into MotiveWave

This tool converts your Trade Like a Dealer — Quantum GEX data string into a MotiveWave-ready Cloud Levels CSV. Follow these steps to go from Quantum data to gradient bands on your chart.

01 Get Quantum Data 02 Convert 03 Level Types 04 Data Format 05 Settings 06 Export to MW
Step 01

SOURCE Getting your Quantum data

01
Log into Trade Like a Dealer
Go to your Trade Like a Dealer account and open the Quantum section. This is where your session GEX levels are generated — it shows the active gamma exposure landscape for the instrument and day you're trading.
02
Select your instrument and session
Choose the instrument you're trading — NQ, MNQ, ES, or MES — and confirm it's the current session date. Quantum updates levels each day so always grab a fresh string before your session.
03
Copy the raw data string
Find and copy the raw data string from Quantum. It's a long semicolon-separated string — copy the entire thing exactly as-is. It will look like this:
Quantum Data String
# Each level separated by semicolons 25200,5,0,25206.9,25193.1,CW,Wall~Range: 14pt~Call: 310.96M~Put: 25.43M; 25000,3,1,25006.2,24993.8,PW,Magnet~Range: 12pt~Call: 63.05M~Put: 67.02M; 24927,0,0,24927,24927,ZG,Zero Gamma~Key pivot where dealer gamma flips
The full string will typically contain 15–25 levels. Copy all of it.
💡
Quantum data is session-specific. Come back each morning before market open and copy a fresh string — levels shift as the options market changes overnight.
Step 02

CONVERT Paste and convert

01
Select your instrument
At the top of the converter click the correct instrument pill — NQ, MNQ, ES, or MES. This sets the symbol column in the CSV so MotiveWave maps the levels to the right chart.
02
Paste your data string
Click into the data input field and paste your Quantum string in full. No editing required — the converter handles all parsing.
03
Click Convert
Hit the Convert button. The stats strip instantly shows your total levels, wall count, magnet count, and the number of CSV rows that will be generated. The levels table and CSV output both populate immediately.
Reference

LEVELS What each level type means

Type Colour What it means How price behaves
WALL Rose Call Wall or Put Wall. Heavy dealer hedging at this strike. Dealers are actively buying or selling delta here to stay hedged as price approaches. Expect stalling, slowdown, or reversal. Strength 4–5 walls are high-conviction barriers. Price often rejects hard or consolidates at these levels.
MAGNET Teal Attractor level. Balanced call/put exposure — dealer gamma is near zero here. Dealers have no strong directional hedging pressure at this strike. Price is drawn toward these levels. Use as intraday targets when price is trending. The nearest magnet often acts as a gravity point before direction is established.
ZG Violet Zero Gamma. The exact strike where dealer gamma flips from positive to negative. The single most important structural level in Quantum data. Above ZG = dealers are short gamma, they amplify moves. Below ZG = dealers are long gamma, they dampen volatility. Trading the flip is a key intraday strategy.
Strength (0–5) reflects notional gamma size at that strike. A strength-5 level has substantially more dealer activity than strength-1 and is more likely to act as a meaningful price barrier. Use the Min/Max Strength sliders to filter the noise and focus only on the tiers that matter for your session plan.

FORMAT Understanding the data string

Each entry in the Quantum string is comma-separated. Entries are separated by semicolons. Here's what each field position means:
Field Map
# Format: strike, strength, isMagnet, upper, lower, type, description 25200 , 5 , 0 , 25206.9 , 25193.1 , CW , Wall~Range: 14pt~Call: 310.96M~Put: 25.43M ↑ ↑ ↑ ↑ ↑ ↑ Strike Str Mag Upper Lower Type
Field 1 — Strike
Price Level
The price this gamma exposure is centred on. Whole or decimal number.
Field 2 — Strength
0 – 5 Rating
Notional significance of this level. 5 is the strongest, 0 the weakest.
Field 3 — isMagnet
0 or 1
1 = Magnet (attractor). 0 = Wall or Zero Gamma.
Field 4 — Upper
Top Band Edge
Upper boundary of the gamma field around this strike price.
Field 5 — Lower
Bottom Band Edge
Lower boundary of the gamma field around this strike price.
Field 6 — Type
CW / PW / ZG
CW = Call Wall, PW = Put Wall, ZG = Zero Gamma pivot.
Step 03

TUNE Adjusting the settings

▲▼
Min / Max Strength
Filter to a specific strength tier. Min 3 / Max 5 shows only high-conviction levels for a clean chart. Min 0 / Max 5 includes everything. Good for busy sessions where you want to declutter without losing the key levels.
↔
Field Scale
Controls band width relative to the Quantum upper/lower bounds. 0.5 (default) is a good starting point. Increase to 1.0–1.5 on higher timeframes where you want wider, more visible bands.
◐
Gradient
Outer Transparency — how faded the band edges are (higher = more transparent). Inner Transparency — opacity near the centre strike. Smoothness — gradient rows per level. Default 30 is a good balance. 50 = ultra smooth but larger CSV. 15 = faster for large level sets.
⚠️
MotiveWave has a row limit for Cloud Levels imports. If you get an error importing, reduce Smoothness to 15–20 or set Min Strength to 2+ to reduce the CSV row count shown in the stats strip.
Step 04

EXPORT Getting levels into MotiveWave

A
Option A — Direct CSV paste
Click Copy CSV to copy the output to clipboard. In MotiveWave go to Add Study → Cloud Levels and paste. Quickest method for one-off sessions.
B
Option B — Download and import file
Click ⬇ Download to save a dated .csv to your computer. In MotiveWave use Add Study → Cloud Levels → Import file and select it.
C
Option C — Live URL (auto-reload)
Upload your CSV to Dropbox (copy the raw share link) or as a GitHub raw file. Paste that URL into the MotiveWave Direct Import URL field at the bottom of the converter — then copy it into MotiveWave's Cloud Levels URL field. MotiveWave will auto-reload your levels whenever the hosted file changes.
✦
Best workflow: host the CSV on Dropbox, paste a fresh Quantum string each morning, download the new CSV, replace the file on Dropbox — MotiveWave picks up the update automatically. No manual chart changes needed.
Selecting specific levels to export
ALL
Quick select by type
Use the All / Walls / Magnets / ZG / None buttons above the table to batch-select a level type. The CSV output updates live — you see the exact row count before downloading.
☑
Click rows to select individually
Click any row to toggle it. Shift+click selects a range. Hover over a row to see the full tooltip with Call $, Put $, and range. Click the strike price cell specifically to copy that number to clipboard without affecting selection.
